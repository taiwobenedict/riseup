Rebased contract.

The rebase algorithm calculates the necessary adjustment to the token's total supply based on deviations from the target price. If the token's price is above the target price, indicating excess demand, the contract increases the total supply through a positive rebase. Conversely, if the price is below the target, indicating excess supply, the contract decreases the total supply through a negative rebase.

The rebase contract operates on a predefined rebase period, which can range from minutes to days. At the end of each period, the contract triggers a rebase.

The contract relies on a price oracle to regularly fetch the token's price from external sources, such as decentralized exchanges (DEXs) or centralized exchanges. This price serves as the basis for calculating the rebase.
